Let's Talk Health

Let’s Talk Health is an advocacy group centered on problems in relation to health. This group targets any issue associated to health such as dissolving the stigma around it, broadening accessibility and creating a space for those who require help and are willing to provide help. Their main focus is to create a campus action plan to be able to improve mental help on campus as a whole. Let’s Talk Health regularly holds Town Hall meetings inviting both students on campus and people off campus to address problems and help devise solutions for any concerns regarding health and wellness at the University of Toronto.

There are no upcoming events for the remainder of the 2013/2014 school year.

Contact: letstalkhealth.uoft@gmail.com

UTSU Mental Health Committee

UTSU Mental Health Committee
The UTSU Mental Health Committee is an advocacy group of around 20 diverse individuals working to make improvements on the university’s mental health record based on the needs ot U of T students. This group holds monthly meetings to work towards three main goals:
1. Promotion of mental health resources: helping students understand health and wellness
2. Improving perception of mental health :anti stigma approach of building on strengths
3. Reforming Campus Health Policy: focus sessions and creating a survey about mental health perceptions

Contact info Kaleem.hala@mail.utoronto.ca

Blue Space Committee

The Blue Space Committee is a new campaign that is promoting the awareness of mental health on campus. This campaign has a goal to break down and remove the stigma that is associated with mental health.

“The word Blue was chosen for a few reasons, but a main reason was to challenge the term ‘feeling blue’ and its relationship with feeling down or sad. A lot of people say that the colour blue also makes them feel calm, peaceful, or full of joy – which connects the different shades of blue to different feelings and emotions that are all a part of a person’s mental health. We want to have Blue Spaces across campus to invite all members of our community to feel welcome, to find support and to talk. It’s a simple way to decrease the stigma related to mental health.”

Contact: Dan Johnson deardan.johnson@utoronto.ca
